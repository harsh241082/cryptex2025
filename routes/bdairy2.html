Bunny's Diary

Today started off the same as always, barely made it to work on time, spent the day wading through emails and meetings. Nothing new, nothing exciting. Just another day in the cycle. Except something different happened. I was walking back from the subway, earbuds in, lost in my own head, when I saw this little girl standing alone near the park entrance. She was probably seven or eight. She was looking around, eyes wide, clearly searching for someone, or something? I took off my earbuds and out of curiosity, just walked up to her. I asked if she was okay. She looked up at me and said, “I lost my brother.” Her voice was small. I looked around. The park wasn’t too crowded, Thought I’ll help out an innocent soul. We walked over together, and I asked her what he looked like. “He’s wearing a blue hoodie. He always runs ahead of me.” She sounded frustrated.

This probably wasn’t the first time her brother had done this. Half an hour went by, but we couldn’t get sight of him. She finally left home, tears welling in her eyes, assuming that her brother must’ve gone back home without her. For some reason I felt a pang in my heart. I hope he’s alright. I hope she’s alright. To be honest, it just felt like a small moment in an otherwise ordinary day. But for some reason, it stuck with me.

I don’t know why. I don’t know. It felt... different.
